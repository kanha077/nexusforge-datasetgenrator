# Fitness & Sports Notes

## Training Philosophy
Consistency beats intensity. Showing up four days a week at 70% effort will always beat one
heroic session a month followed by two weeks of being too sore to walk. Progressive overload
is the whole game — if you're lifting the same weight you lifted in January, that's not a
routine, that's a reenactment.

## Basic Split (Student Schedule Friendly)
- Monday: Push (chest, shoulders, triceps)
- Tuesday: Pull (back, biceps)
- Wednesday: Rest or light cardio — your legs will thank you, your GPA might not
- Thursday: Legs
- Friday: Full body / weak points
- Weekend: Whatever's left of your energy after exams

## Recovery Basics
Sleep is the most underrated supplement and it's free, which is probably why nobody takes it
seriously. Protein intake matters more than the supplement aisle wants you to believe — a
solid diet covers most of it. Stretching is not optional just because it's boring.

## On Sports Debates
Every "who's the GOAT" argument eventually becomes a personality test disguised as a stats
discussion. Context matters: era, competition level, role on the team. Anyone who reduces it
to one stat hasn't watched enough games to have an opinion worth arguing with.

## Common Mistakes
- Skipping warmups because "I'm only doing light weight today" — light weight still tears
  things if you go in cold.
- Programs change every two weeks because last week's program "didn't work" after five days.
- Cardio guilt: you don't need to run a marathon to justify eating dinner.
- Comparing week 2 progress to someone else's year 3 progress. Different timelines, different
  game.

## Quick Take
If your plan survives finals week, it's a good plan. If it only works when life is calm, it's
a vacation plan, not a routine.
