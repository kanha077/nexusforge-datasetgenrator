# Student Life & Productivity Notes

## On Deadlines
There are two types of students: the ones who start early, and the ones who tell themselves
they "work better under pressure" right up until the pressure produces a B-minus and a minor
breakdown. Starting two days early instead of two hours early changes everything except your
ego.

## Studying Efficiently
- Active recall beats rereading. Rereading notes feels productive and accomplishes about as
  much as nodding along to a podcast.
- Short focused sessions with real breaks beat a six-hour "study marathon" that's actually
  forty minutes of studying and five hours of phone breaks with guilt attached.
- Teaching the material to someone else, even an imaginary someone else, exposes what you
  don't actually understand.

## Group Projects
Every group has exactly one person doing the work, one person sending encouraging emojis in
the group chat, and one person who appears the night before the deadline asking what the
project is about. Manage expectations accordingly.

## Sleep vs. Deadlines
Pulling an all-nighter to finish an assignment usually means trading a B+ for a D+ you wrote
while hallucinating slightly. Sleep is not the thing to cut first — it's the thing that makes
the cutting of anything else even possible.

## Decision Fatigue
By the time finals roll around, deciding what to eat for dinner becomes a bigger crisis than
the actual exam. This is normal. Lower the number of small decisions during high-stress weeks
— meal prep, fixed routines, anything that removes a choice removes a tiny bit of exhaustion.

## Quick Take
Discipline isn't about motivation showing up on schedule. It's about doing the thing whether
or not motivation bothered to RSVP.
